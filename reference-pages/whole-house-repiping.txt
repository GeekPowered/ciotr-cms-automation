## Signs You Need Whole Home Repiping

Discolored water coming from your faucets or fixtures

Low water pressure throughout your plumbing system

Frequent leaks or repeated plumbing repairs

Mold growth or water damage behind walls and ceilings

Older homes with galvanized steel or copper pipes showing signs of corrosion

## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know if my home needs repiping?
A: If you’re experiencing discolored water, low water pressure, frequent leaks, or have Kitec pipes in an older home, it’s time to consider repiping.

Q: What materials are best for repiping?
A: PEX pipes are cost-effective and flexible, while copper pipes are long-lasting and resistant to UV light. Our team will help you choose the best material for your home.

Q: How long does a whole-home repiping project take?
A: Most projects take just a few days, depending on the size of the house and the complexity of the plumbing system. We’ll provide a clear timeline during your consultation.

Q: Will repiping damage my walls?
A: Our plumbers work carefully to minimize disruption. We only open small access points as needed and have great referrals for wall repair services.
