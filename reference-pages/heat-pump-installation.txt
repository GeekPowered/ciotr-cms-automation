## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know if a heat pump is right for my Austin home?
A: A heat pump is an efficient alternative to traditional heating and cooling systems, making it ideal for many Austin homes. Because it transfers heat from the outside air rather than generating it directly, it can provide reliable comfort year-round while improving energy efficiency.

Q: How does a heat pump work in both heating and cooling modes?
A: A heat pump system moves heat rather than creating it. In cooling mode, it removes heat from inside your home and releases it through the outdoor units. In heating mode, it extracts heat from the outside air and transfers it indoors, providing efficient heating even during cooler months.

Q: Will I still need backup heat with a heat pump?
A: Most modern heat pump systems include backup heat to support the heating system during extreme cold snaps. While Austin’s climate is generally mild, backup heat ensures consistent comfort when temperatures drop unexpectedly.

Q: What environmental benefits do heat pumps provide?
A: Heat pumps offer significant environmental benefits because they use less energy than traditional heating systems and reduce carbon emissions. By improving energy efficiency and lowering energy use, they provide an environmentally friendly solution for heating and cooling your home.

Q: How can I tell if my heat pump needs repair?
A: Common signs your heat pump needs repair include unusual noises, uneven heating, weak airflow, rising energy bills, or the system struggling to maintain temperature. If you notice these issues, prompt heat pump repair can restore efficiency and prevent further damage.
